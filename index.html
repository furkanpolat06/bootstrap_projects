What is physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession that focuses on restoring and improving physical function and mobility through manual techniques, exercise, and education. It helps individuals recover from injuries, manage pain, and enhance overall well-being

What does a physiotherapist do?

A physiotherapist helps individuals improve their physical function, mobility, and overall well-being through various techniques and treatments.

WHat is exercise ?

Exercise refers to physical activity performed with the intention of improving or maintaining physical fitness, health, and overall well-being.

GENERAL PHYSIOTHERAPY

Assessment and Diagnosis

Physical therapy involves assessing and diagnosing the patient's condition through various tests and evaluations. This helps the therapist understand the root cause of the problem and develop an appropriate treatment plan.

Treatment and Intervention

Once the assessment is complete, physical therapists utilize a variety of techniques and interventions to help patients recover and regain function. These may include exercises, manual therapy, electrotherapy, and other modalities tailored to the individual's needs

Prevention and Education

Physical therapists also play a crucial role in preventing injuries and promoting overall wellness. They educate patients about proper body mechanics, ergonomics, and exercise techniques to prevent future problems and maintain a healthy lifestyle.

neurological rehabilitation

Neurological Physiotherapy

Neurological physiotherapy is a branch of physiotherapy that specializes in treating individuals with neurological conditions, focusing on improving their movement and functional abilities

Stroke Rehabilitation

Neurological physiotherapists help individuals recover mobility and regain independence after a stroke through targeted exercises and therapies.

Spinal Cord Injury Rehabilitation

Physiotherapy for spinal cord injuries focuses on enhancing strength, mobility, and functional abilities for individuals with spinal cord damage.

Parkinson's Disease Management

Neurophysiotherapists employ exercises and movement strategies to improve mobility, balance, and reduce symptoms such as muscle rigidity and tremors in individuals with Parkinson's disease.

Multiple Sclerosis (MS) Rehabilitation

Neurophysiotherapy aims to enhance physical function and manage symptoms related to multiple sclerosis through exercises, strategies to manage fatigue, and optimize daily activities.

Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) Rehabilitation

Physiotherapists assist individuals with traumatic brain injuries in recovering motor skills, coordination, and independence in daily activities, while addressing cognitive and communication impairments.

Sports Rehabilitation

Sports physiotherapy focuses on preventing and treating sports-related injuries in athletes and active individuals, utilizing specialized techniques and exercises to optimize performance and facilitate safe recovery and return to sports activities. It involves a holistic approach that addresses not only the injury but also the athlete's overall physical fitness and well-being. Sports physiotherapy also involves rehabilitation and recovery management, aiming to promote tissue healing, restore function, and prevent re-injury, allowing athletes to regain their peak performance level in a timely and safe manner.
